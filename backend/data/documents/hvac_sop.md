# HVAC Standard Operating Procedures

## SOP-001: High Supply Air Temperature Investigation

### Purpose
This SOP provides step-by-step instructions for investigating and resolving high supply air temperature alarms on Air Handling Units (AHUs).

### Scope
Applicable to all air handling units in the building management system.

### Procedure

#### Step 1: Verify the Alarm
1. Check the BMS for the current supply air temperature reading
2. Compare against the setpoint (typically 55°F / 12.8°C)
3. Verify the sensor is reading correctly by comparing with a portable thermometer

#### Step 2: Check Chilled Water System
1. Verify chilled water supply temperature (should be 42-44°F)
2. Check chilled water valve position (should be modulating open)
3. Verify chilled water flow rate through the coil
4. Check differential pressure across the chilled water coil

#### Step 3: Inspect the Cooling Coil
1. Check for dirty or clogged coil fins
2. Verify there are no air locks in the coil
3. Check coil leaving air temperature
4. Inspect condensate drain pan for blockage

#### Step 4: Check Fan Operation
1. Verify supply fan is running at correct speed
2. Check fan belt tension and condition
3. Verify VFD is operating correctly
4. Check motor amperage against nameplate

#### Step 5: Review Filter Status
1. Check filter differential pressure
2. Replace filters if DP exceeds threshold
3. Verify filter rack is properly sealed

### Resolution
Document all findings in the maintenance log. If the issue persists after all steps, escalate to the facilities engineering team.

## SOP-002: Chiller High Condenser Pressure Response

### Purpose
Emergency response procedure for high condenser pressure on centrifugal chillers.

### Immediate Actions
1. **DO NOT** manually override safety controls
2. Check cooling tower fan operation
3. Verify condenser water flow rate
4. Monitor condenser pressure trend

### Investigation Steps
1. Check ambient wet bulb temperature
2. Inspect condenser tubes for fouling
3. Verify cooling tower water treatment chemistry
4. Check for non-condensable gases in the refrigerant circuit
5. Verify refrigerant charge level

### Preventive Measures
- Schedule annual condenser tube cleaning
- Maintain water treatment program
- Perform quarterly refrigerant analysis
- Keep cooling tower fill media clean

## SOP-003: VAV Box Troubleshooting

### Purpose
Systematic troubleshooting procedure for Variable Air Volume box issues.

### Common Issues
1. **Damper Stuck Open/Closed**
   - Check actuator operation
   - Verify control signal (0-10VDC or 4-20mA)
   - Inspect mechanical linkage
   - Test actuator stroke manually

2. **Insufficient Airflow**
   - Check upstream static pressure
   - Verify minimum airflow setpoint
   - Inspect air filter at box inlet
   - Check for duct leaks

3. **Temperature Control Issues**
   - Verify zone temperature sensor
   - Check reheat valve/coil operation
   - Review control sequence
   - Check for solar load or internal heat gains

## SOP-004: Boiler Low Water Level Emergency

### Purpose
Emergency procedure for boiler low water level conditions.

### CRITICAL SAFETY WARNING
**Never add water to a boiler with an unknown water level. This can cause a steam explosion.**

### Immediate Actions
1. Shut down the boiler immediately
2. Close the main steam valve
3. Do NOT attempt to add water
4. Notify the facilities manager
5. Contact a licensed boiler technician

### Investigation (After Cooldown)
1. Check feed water pump operation
2. Inspect water level controls
3. Test low water cutoff device
4. Check for steam or water leaks
5. Verify makeup water supply
